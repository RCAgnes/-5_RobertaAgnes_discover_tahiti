Discover Tahiti

Your guide to the beauty, culture, and gastronomy of French Polynesia

Welcome to Tahiti

Tahiti, the largest island in French Polynesia, is renowned for its turquoise lagoons, overwater bungalows, and vibrant culture. From lush mountains to crystal-clear beaches, Tahiti offers a tropical paradise with a rich mix of Polynesian and French influences.

Culture and Traditions

Tahitian culture is deeply rooted in tradition. Tattoos are a sacred practice representing identity and genealogy. Dance is a powerful cultural expression, often performed during ceremonies and festivities. Visitors will also experience the legendary hospitality of the Polynesian people.

Gastronomy

Tahitian cuisine is a delightful blend of fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and French flavors. One of the most traditional dishes is Poisson Cru (raw fish marinated in lime juice and coconut milk). Meals are often served in a relaxed and communal style.

Travel Tips

The best time to visit Tahiti is between May and October during the dry season. Don’t miss visiting the islands of Moorea and Bora Bora. Make sure to learn a few words in Tahitian or French – locals appreciate the effort!
